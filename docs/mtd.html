MTD FAQ

Can I really do MTD with a spreadsheet?

Yes. HMRC officially permits spreadsheet + bridging software. The bridging software is what connects your spreadsheet to HMRC's submission API. There are free bridging tools and paid ones from about £15 to £30 one-time. The MTD Spreadsheet pack includes a shortlist.

Do I really need to submit four times a year?

Yes if your income is over the threshold for your phase. The quarterly updates are cumulative totals, not full tax returns. The full tax return ("final declaration") is still annual.

What happens if I miss a quarterly update?

Penalty points. Each missed submission earns a point. Hit 4 points and you get a £200 fine. Points expire after 24 months of clean submissions. Use the Penalty Clock if you want the dramatic version.

Will incorporating to a Ltd company avoid MTD?

Not really. Companies have their own MTD regime arriving later. Incorporation has its own administrative overhead. The numbers on the trade-off live in our Freelancer Toolkit.

Is the MTD Spreadsheet HMRC-recognised?

The spreadsheet itself is a template you own. The bridging software that submits to HMRC is HMRC-recognised. We list current HMRC-recognised bridging tools inside the pack and keep that list updated.
