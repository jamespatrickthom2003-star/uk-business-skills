The 12 MTD terms HMRC writes badly.

Plain English. 40 words each. One worked example each. The thing accountants charge you to translate.

How to read these cards. Term + the official HMRC name. Then the plain meaning. Then a worked example using a real number. Built from GOV.UK MTD ITSA guidance + ICAEW TAXguide 01/25.
01
HMRC term

Qualifying income

Your gross self-employment income plus your gross property income, added together, before any expenses. If this combined number is over the threshold for your phase, you are in MTD.

Worked example£38,000 from freelance design + £14,000 from one rental = £52,000 qualifying income. Above the £50k threshold. You are mandated from 6 April 2026.
02
HMRC term

Cumulative update

A quarterly submission to HMRC showing your year-to-date totals (not just the quarter). Q3 shows Q1+Q2+Q3 totals, not the three-month-only numbers. You correct earlier mistakes by overwriting.

Worked exampleQ1 turnover £9k. Q2 turnover £7k. Your Q2 submission sends £16k cumulative, not £7k. Forgot a Q1 invoice? Add it to the cumulative total in Q2, no resubmit needed.
03
HMRC term

EOPS (End of Period Statement)

Originally a fifth annual submission to confirm your trading year is closed. HMRC scrapped the separate EOPS step in late 2024. Now folded into the Final Declaration. If you read older guidance saying EOPS, skip past it.

Worked exampleOld plan: 4 quarterly updates + 1 EOPS + 1 final declaration = 6 submissions/year. New plan: 4 quarterly updates + 1 final declaration = 5. Easier.
04
HMRC term

Final Declaration

The annual confirmation, due 31 January after the tax year ends. Replaces the old Self Assessment return for MTD-mandated people. Confirms your total income, allowances, and tax due. Quarterly updates feed into this.

Worked example2026-27 tax year ends 5 April 2027. Final Declaration due 31 January 2028. By then HMRC already has your Q1-Q4 updates, so the final declaration is a confirmation plus any other income (savings, dividends, etc.).
05
HMRC term

Bridging software

The connector between your spreadsheet and HMRC's submission API. You keep your records in Excel/Numbers/Sheets. The bridging software reads the numbers and submits them. HMRC officially recognises this path.

Worked exampleFree options: 123 Sheets free tier, VitalTax free. Paid one-time: BTC Software, Easy MTD around £15-£30. No subscription needed if you stay on a spreadsheet.
06
HMRC term

Compatible software

Any software HMRC has certified as able to submit to MTD. Includes the SaaS accounting tools (Sage, Xero, QB) AND the bridging tools above. "Compatible" does not mean "you need a subscription". Spreadsheet + bridging = compatible.

Worked exampleHMRC's "find software" page lists 30+ compatible options. About 25 are paid SaaS subscriptions. The other 5-7 are bridging tools that work with your spreadsheet. Pick the path that suits you.
07
HMRC term

Quarterly update

Each of the four times-per-year submissions. Quarter 1 covers 6 April to 5 July, submitted by 7 August. Q2 to 5 October submitted by 7 November. Q3 to 5 January submitted by 7 February. Q4 to 5 April submitted by 7 May.

Worked example2026 dates: Q1 due 7 Aug 2026, Q2 due 7 Nov 2026, Q3 due 7 Feb 2027, Q4 due 7 May 2027, Final Declaration due 31 Jan 2028.
08
HMRC term

Reasonable excuse

The defence against penalties for a missed submission. HMRC's "reasonable excuse" bar is narrow: serious illness, bereavement, IT failure of HMRC's own systems, etc. "I forgot" and "I was busy" do NOT count.

Worked exampleHospitalised the week of a quarterly deadline = reasonable excuse, appeal succeeds. Software subscription lapsed and you couldn't submit = not reasonable, penalty stands.
09
HMRC term

Soft-landing period

A grace window where HMRC will not enforce penalties even if you submit late. There is NO soft-landing for MTD ITSA in 2026. The penalty regime starts on day one. (There was one for MTD VAT in 2019, hence the term.)

Worked exampleMiss Q1 on 7 Aug 2026 = penalty point starts immediately. Do not bank on a grace period this time.
10
HMRC term

Time to pay arrangement

A formal payment plan with HMRC if you cannot pay the tax due. Set up online if you owe under £30k. Spreads the bill over up to 12 months. Does not stop interest accruing but stops enforcement action.

Worked exampleFinal Declaration shows you owe £4,200. Cannot pay in full. Set up a 6-month time-to-pay arrangement online: £700/month, interest on the balance, no bailiffs.
11
HMRC term

Combined gross income

The qualifying income test for MTD looks at your self-employment AND property income together. Even if neither alone hits the threshold, the combined sum can. This catches a lot of side-hustle freelancers with a rental property.

Worked example£35k freelance + £18k from one property = £53k combined. Both individually below £50k. Combined above. You are in.
12
HMRC term

Voluntary sign-up

Joining MTD before your phase mandates it. You can sign up now even if your income is under £50k. Some accountants suggest this; most do not. The main reason to do it: spread the learning curve before your mandatory date.

Worked exampleYou earn £22k freelance. Your mandate date is 6 April 2028. Voluntary sign-up now means 2 years of practice before it counts. Some people prefer the deadline pressure. Trade-off.
