The problem: 2.65 million UK landlords are not prepared

The Renters' Rights Act 2025 is the biggest change to UK landlord law in decades. Section 21 is gone. All tenancies are now periodic. Every possession claim must use one of 37 specified grounds. Serving the wrong notice, on the wrong ground, with the wrong notice period, or without completing the compliance pre-conditions invalidates the entire claim.

  • Serving a Section 8 notice with the wrong notice period invalidates it, start again
  • Missing a compliance step (deposit protection, information sheet, gas certificate) blocks a possession claim entirely
  • Operating without a required licence now risks £40,000 fines and 24-month Rent Repayment Orders
  • The new Decent Homes Standard and Awaab's Law extension add new obligations from 2026
