## 1. Section 8 Notices — Key Grounds for Possession

From 1 May 2026, Section 8 is the **sole route** for possession proceedings. Section 21 no-fault evictions are abolished.

### 12-Month Protected Period
Grounds 1, 1A, and 6 cannot be used within the first 12 months of the tenancy.

### Mandatory Grounds (Court must grant possession if proven)

| Ground | Description | Notice Period |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **1** | Landlord or family member requires the property as principal home | 4 months |
| **1A** | Landlord intends to sell the property | 4 months |
| **6** | Landlord intends to demolish, reconstruct, or carry out substantial works | 4 months |
| **8** | Serious rent arrears — at least 3 months' rent unpaid | 4 weeks |
| **8A** | Repeated rent arrears — 2+ months on at least 3 occasions in 3 years | 4 weeks |

### Discretionary Grounds (Court may grant possession if reasonable)

| Ground | Description | Notice Period |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **10** | Some rent arrears (any amount) | 4 weeks |
| **11** | Persistent delay in paying rent | 4 weeks |
| **12** | Breach of tenancy obligation | 2 weeks |
| **14** | Nuisance, annoyance, or illegal use | 2 weeks |

## 2. Key Changes Under Renters' Rights Act 2025

From 1 May 2026:
- **All new tenancies are periodic from day one.** Fixed-term ASTs are abolished.
- **Tenants may end a periodic tenancy** by giving **2 months' notice** at any time.
- **Landlords** must use Section 8 grounds to end a tenancy.
- **Deposit cap:** 5 weeks' rent (annual rent below £50,000) or 6 weeks' rent.
